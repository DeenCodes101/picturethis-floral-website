Plant Care Guide

Everything you need to keep your healing plants happy and healthy

Watering

Most bedroom plants prefer soil that dries slightly between waterings. Overwatering is the most common mistake.

Light

Place plants near bright, indirect light. Avoid harsh afternoon sun directly on leaves.

Humidity

Ferns and tropical plants love humidity. Mist leaves weekly or use a pebble tray with water.

Aloe Vera

  • Water every 2–3 weeks; allow soil to dry completely between waterings.
  • Place in bright, indirect sunlight near a window.
  • Use well-draining cactus or succulent potting mix.
  • Ideal temperature: 55–80°F (13–27°C).

Areca Palm

  • Keep soil consistently moist but not soggy.
  • Thrives in bright, filtered light; avoid direct sun.
  • Mist regularly to maintain humidity above 40%.
  • Fertilize monthly during spring and summer.

Boston Fern

  • Water when the top inch of soil feels dry.
  • Prefers indirect light and shaded corners.
  • High humidity is essential — mist daily if needed.
  • Trim brown fronds to encourage new growth.

English Ivy

  • Water moderately; let soil dry slightly between waterings.
  • Grows well in low to medium indirect light.
  • Keep away from pets — ivy can be toxic if ingested.
  • Prune regularly to control trailing growth.

Gardenia

  • Keep soil evenly moist; never let it dry out completely.
  • Needs 4–6 hours of bright, indirect light daily.
  • Prefers acidic soil and warm temperatures (65–70°F).
  • Remove spent blooms to encourage new flowers.

Jasmine

  • Water when the top layer of soil is dry to the touch.
  • Place in a sunny spot with at least 4 hours of light.
  • Support vines with a trellis or stake as they grow.
  • Blooms best in cooler night temperatures.

Lavender

  • Water sparingly — lavender dislikes wet roots.
  • Requires full sun for at least 6 hours a day.
  • Use sandy, well-draining soil.
  • Prune after flowering to maintain shape.

Orchid

  • Water once a week by soaking roots for 10–15 minutes.
  • Bright, indirect eastern or western window light is ideal.
  • Use orchid-specific potting mix with bark chips.
  • Repot every 1–2 years after blooming finishes.

Spider Plant

  • Water when soil feels dry; tolerant of occasional neglect.
  • Adapts to most light conditions, prefers bright indirect.
  • Produces baby plantlets — propagate by planting in new pots.
  • Feed with diluted fertilizer monthly in growing season.
